Who was Herbert Marshall Mcluhan?

Famous for coining the expression "the medium is the message" and the term global village which is our internet and world wide web today, 35 years before it was invented. McLuhan theorized that the medium that carries a message becomes a part of the message itself and influences how people perceive it.

Born in Edmonton, Canada, he would have turned 106 on July 21st this year. Mcluhan studied at the University of Manitoba and the University of Cambridge. He then taught at a few different universities before becoming a full professor at the University of Toronto. In the 1960s, he was known as a media theorist and his vision of a "global village". Mcluhan predicted that the world was entering the age in which the community would be connected by technology and everyone had access to the information through this technology. He became a TV personality and was the most publicised English teacher of the 20th century. His theories were met with controversy yet influential even until after his death in 1980. Then in 1989, when the internet was born, interest heighten in his work and perspective again.

One of his preeminent predictions included the four eras of human history.

  • The acoustic age
  • The literary age
  • The print age
  • The electronic age

Other published work included:

  • The Mechanical Bride (1951)
  • The Gutenberg Galaxy (1962)
  • Understanding Media (1964)
  • The Medium is the Massage: An Inventory of Effects (1967)
  • War and Peace in the Global Village (1968)
  • From Cliche to Archetype (1970)
  • The Global Village: Transformations in World Life and Media in the 21st Century (1989)
